How Rights & Royalties Work

Ownership

Artists keep ownership of their music. Skaters keep ownership of their performances. Roll Show receives a license to use, distribute, and monetize content on our platform and on third‑party services (like Tidal, Viva, and others).

Recorder Rights

By default, the person who records the video owns the recording. During skater signup and upload, we explain this clearly and give skaters tools to:

  • Identify who recorded the clip.
  • Give the recorder a percentage if they agree.
  • Document that agreement in the platform.

40 / 40 / 20 Split

For eligible content, net revenue is split:

  • 40% to the artist (music).
  • 40% to the skater (performance/video).
  • 20% to Roll Show (platform/label share).

If a recorder is added, we make it easy to assign them a percentage and adjust the split.

No Upfront Fees

Roll Show operates as a no‑upfront indie label and platform. We don’t charge artists or skaters to join. We earn only from our share of royalties and platform revenue.
